# Void Loss Analysis — Key Findings & Recommendations
**Audience:** Senior Management Team | **Period:** April 2025 – March 2026 (11 months in dataset; February 2026 not included)  
**Organisational target (2025/26):** 6.5%

---

## Executive summary (say this first)

Organisationally we are **not meeting** the 6.5% void loss target. Year-to-date void loss is **£844k** against **£7.83m** rent charged (**10.78%**), which is **4.3 percentage points above target** — roughly **£335k** of avoidable void loss if we had performed at target. There are **signs of improvement** in recent months (organisationally around **10.5%** in the last three months vs **11.6%** in Apr–Jun), but performance remains materially off target and uneven by specialism and building.

## Recommendations

### 1. Immediate operational focus (2025/26 year remainder)

1. **Void loss SWAT on top 5 buildings** — agree 90-day void reduction plans with responsible managers; track weekly void days and re-let times.  
2. **Homelessness programme review** — 41% of void loss £; prioritise Hopley Road, Sherry Close, and other outliers.  
3. **Replicate August / December conditions** — analyse what drove org months at **8.8%** and **9.8%** (staffing, void pipeline, seasonal lets) and codify into standard work.  
4. **Manager peer support** — pair Managers 6/8 with Managers 2–4 on void turnaround good practice.  

### 2. Reporting & governance

1. Maintain **YTD % and £** as the headline KPI; add **in-month %** and **3-month rolling average** to show direction without over-reacting to single months.  
2. Report **specialism YTD** and **top 10 buildings by void loss £** monthly to SMT.  
3. Clarify **February 2026** data gap before board reporting.  

### 3. Recommended organisational target for **2026/27**

**Recommended organisational target: 9.0%** (with a **stretch** internal ambition of **8.5%** by Q4 2026/27).

**Rationale:**

| Factor | Evidence |
|--------|----------|
| Current YTD (25/26) | 10.78% — too high to repeat as target |
| Original ambition | 6.5% — still valid long-term but **not credible** as a single-year step from ~11% |
| Recent achievable performance | Aug 25 **8.8%**; trailing 6 months **~10.6%** |
| Realistic improvement | ~1.5–1.8 pp reduction from YTD/run-rate over 12 months |

**Suggested specialism targets for 2026/27** (aligned to portfolio challenge, not uniform cut):

| Specialism              | Suggested 26/27 target |
|-------------------------|-------------------------|
| Mental Health           | **7.5%**                |
| Young People            | **8.5%**                |
| Homelessness            | **10.0%**               |
| Learning Disabilities   | **10.5%**               |

**Phased pathway:** Plan to return to **6.5% organisationally by 2028/29** through annual step-downs (e.g. 9.0% → 7.5% → 6.5%) rather than one aggressive step.
